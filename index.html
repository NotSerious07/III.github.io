History at a Glance

Key milestones from 1916 to the electric era.

1916–1917
Founding of BMW from Rapp Motorenwerke; early focus on aircraft engines. The blue‑white roundel reflects the Bavarian colors.
1923
First BMW motorcycle — the R32 — debuts with a flat‑twin boxer engine layout.
1928–1929
Entry into automobile production via Fahrzeugfabrik Eisenach (Dixi); BMW 3/15 becomes the first BMW‑badged car.
1936
BMW 328 sports car sets a benchmark for lightweight performance and motorsport success.
1955–1959
Post‑war recovery: Isetta microcar keeps BMW afloat; luxury 507 and 503 showcase design ambition.
1962–1972
“Neue Klasse” sedans revive the brand and set the DNA for modern BMW; BMW M GmbH and the iconic headquarters open in 1972.
1975–1989
3 Series launches (E21) and becomes a global best‑seller; the M1 mid‑engine supercar and M3 (E30) cement performance heritage.
1994–2003
Rover Group acquisition and later divestment; MINI relaunch under BMW; Rolls‑Royce Motor Cars joins the group (production from 2003).
2013
BMW i3 (carbon‑fiber EV city car) and i8 (plug‑in hybrid sports car) signal a bold electrified future.
2020s
Expanding the electric lineup — i4, iX, i7 — with continued focus on software, sustainability, and driver engagement.

Motorsport Heritage

From touring cars to Formula One power and Le Mans victory.

Touring & GT

DTM and GT racing forged legends like the M3 and M4. Privateer teams worldwide race BMWs for their balance and durability.

F1 & Endurance

BMW turbo power won the 1983 F1 World Championship with Brabham. In 1999, the V12 LMR secured overall victory at the 24 Hours of Le Mans.
